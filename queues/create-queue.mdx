## What is a queue?

Imagine a bakery. Customers walk in and there is a line. The person at the front gets served first, and everyone else waits their turn. A call queue works exactly the same way, but for phone calls.

When all your agents are busy on calls, new callers join the queue. They hear hold music (instead of standing in a physical line) and wait until someone on your team is free. Then the system automatically connects the next caller in line to that agent.

Without a queue, callers hear a busy tone or get sent to voicemail. With a queue, they wait and always reach a real person.

<Info>
  Queues are perfect for support teams, sales teams, or any group where multiple people answer calls for the same phone number.
</Info>

## How to create a queue

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Queues page">
    In the left sidebar, click **Configure**, then click **Queues**.

    You will see a list of all queues in your organisation. If this is your first queue, the list will be empty.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add Queue">
    Click the **+ Add Queue** button at the top of the page. A form will open up.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Give it a name and number">
    Fill in these two fields:

    - **Queue Name** -- a human-friendly name like "Support Team" or "Sales Line"
    - **Queue Number** -- a short number like `5001` or `5002`

    The queue number is what the system uses internally to route calls to this queue. Think of it like an extension, but for a group of people instead of one person.

    <Tip>
      Use numbers in the 5000 range for queues (5001, 5002, 5003) so you can easily tell them apart from user extensions (which are usually in the 1000 range).
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick a ring strategy">
    The ring strategy decides **who rings when a call comes in**. You have three options:

    | Strategy | What it does | Good for |
    | --- | --- | --- |
    | **Ring All** | Every agent's phone rings at the same time. First person to pick up gets the call. | Small teams where speed matters |
    | **Round Robin** | The system takes turns. Agent A gets the first call, Agent B gets the second, Agent C gets the third, then back to A. | Spreading the work evenly |
    | **Least Recent** | The agent who has not answered a call in the longest time rings first. | Making sure nobody gets overloaded |

    <Info>
      Not sure which to pick? Start with **Ring All**. It is the simplest and makes sure calls get answered as fast as possible.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure queue settings">
    Set these options to control the caller's experience while they wait:

    - **Timeout** -- how many seconds each agent's phone rings before moving on (e.g. 30 seconds)
    - **Max Wait Time** -- the maximum time a caller stays in the queue before being dropped or sent to voicemail (e.g. 300 seconds = 5 minutes)
    - **Music on Hold** -- the audio callers hear while waiting
    - **Greeting** -- a short message callers hear when they first enter the queue (e.g. "All agents are busy. Please hold.")

    <Warning>
      If you set the max wait time too short, callers may get disconnected before an agent has time to answer. We recommend at least 120 seconds (2 minutes) as a minimum.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the queue">
    Click **Save**. Your queue is now created.

    You can now add agents to the queue and route a phone number to it.
  </Step>
</Steps>
