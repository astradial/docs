## What is call history?

Call history is a log of every single phone call in your organisation. Every time someone makes a call, receives a call, misses a call, or a call fails -- it gets recorded here. Think of it like a diary that writes down every phone conversation automatically.

## How to view call history

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Calls page">
    In the sidebar, go to **Monitor** and click **Calls**. This is where all your call logs live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse the table">
    You will see a table with every call. Each row is one call, showing you the key details at a glance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you see in the table

Every call in the table shows these details:

| Column     | What it means                                                    |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **From**   | Who made the call (a phone number or extension)                  |
| **To**     | Who the call was going to                                        |
| **Duration** | How long the call lasted (e.g. 2m 34s)                         |
| **Status** | What happened to the call (answered, missed, etc.)               |
| **Time**   | When the call happened                                           |

## Call statuses

Each call has a status that tells you what happened.

- **Answered** -- the call was picked up and a conversation happened
- **No Answer** -- the call rang but nobody picked up
- **Busy** -- the person being called was already on another call
- **Failed** -- something went wrong and the call could not connect

<Tip>
  If you see a lot of "No Answer" calls, it might mean your team needs more agents or a call queue so callers do not hang up waiting.
</Tip>

## Filtering calls

You do not have to scroll through every single call to find what you need. Use the filter buttons at the top of the page:

- **All** -- shows every call (inbound and outbound)
- **Inbound** -- shows only calls that came in from the outside
- **Outbound** -- shows only calls your team made to the outside

<Info>
  Inbound means someone called you. Outbound means you called someone. Simple as that.
</Info>

## Viewing a call's journey

Want to know exactly what happened during a call? Click on any call in the table to open the **call journey**. This shows you every step the call took from start to finish.

For example, a call journey might look like:

1. Call came in on phone number +91 22 1234 5678
2. Routed to queue "Sales"
3. Rang agent Sarah (ext 1001) -- no answer
4. Rang agent John (ext 1002) -- answered
5. Call ended after 3 minutes 12 seconds

<Note>
  The call journey is especially helpful for troubleshooting. If a caller says "I was transferred around and nobody helped me", you can see exactly what happened step by step.
</Note>

## Who can see what

- **Owners, Admins, and Managers** can see all calls across the organisation
- **Agents** can only see their own calls

<Warning>
  If you are an Agent and do not see a call you expected, it is because your role only lets you see calls where you were directly involved. Ask a Manager or Admin if you need to see other calls.
</Warning>
