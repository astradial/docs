## What is Super Human?

Super Human is the name for Astradial's AI-powered voice bots. These are virtual agents that can answer phone calls and have real conversations with your callers -- just like a person would.

Think of it like hiring a receptionist who never sleeps, never takes a day off, and can handle a hundred calls at the same time. The bot picks up the phone, listens to the caller, talks back, and does things like:

- Answer common questions ("What are your check-in times?")
- Collect information from the caller (name, booking number, etc.)
- Create support tickets automatically
- Transfer the call to a real person when the bot cannot help

Super Human bots work 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Callers talk to them just like they would talk to a human, using their normal voice.

<Info>
  The "Super Human" name is what you see in the dashboard. You might also hear these called "AI bots" or "AI agents" -- they all mean the same thing.
</Info>

## How to create a bot

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Super Human page">
    In the left sidebar, click **Configure**, then click **Super Human**.

    You will see a list of all AI bots in your organisation. If you have not created any yet, the list will be empty.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new bot">
    Click the **+ Create Bot** button. A setup form will open.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Give it a name">
    Enter a name for your bot. Pick something that describes what it does.

    **Examples:**
    - "Front Desk Bot" -- for a hotel reception bot
    - "Support Bot" -- for a customer support bot
    - "After Hours Bot" -- for a bot that handles calls outside business hours

    <Tip>
      The name is just for you and your team. Callers never see or hear the bot's name.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select an AI model">
    Choose which AI model powers your bot's brain. Different models have different strengths -- some are faster, some are smarter, some are better at conversations.

    Your Astradial admin will have configured which models are available for your organisation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure the conversation flow">
    This is where you tell the bot what to say and how to behave. You can set up:

    - **System prompt** -- instructions that tell the bot who it is and how to act (e.g. "You are a friendly front desk agent at Grand Estancia Hotel")
    - **Tools** -- actions the bot can take during a call (create a ticket, transfer the call, look up information)
    - **Conversation flow** -- the step-by-step path the bot follows during a call

    <Note>
      The conversation flow is built using a visual editor. You do not need to write any code. Just drag, drop, and connect the steps.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the bot">
    Click **Save**. Your bot is now created but not yet connected to a phone number.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connect a bot to a phone number

A bot sitting by itself does nothing. You need to tell the system: "When someone calls this number, let the bot answer."

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Phone Numbers">
    In the left sidebar, click **Deploy**, then click **Phone Numbers**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open routing for your number">
    Find the phone number you want the bot to answer. Click the **three-dot menu** (three dots icon) on that number's row and select **Configure Routing**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select AI Agent">
    In the routing type dropdown, pick **AI Agent**.

    A second dropdown appears showing all your bots. Select the bot you just created.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. That is it.

    The next time someone calls this phone number, your AI bot will pick up and start talking.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Make sure your bot's conversation flow is fully set up before connecting it to a live phone number. An incomplete bot might confuse callers.
</Warning>

## What can bots do?

Here is a quick summary of everything a Super Human bot can handle during a call:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Answer questions" icon="comments">
    The bot can answer frequently asked questions based on the information you give it. No need for callers to wait on hold for simple questions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Create tickets" icon="ticket">
    When a caller has an issue, the bot can automatically create a support ticket with all the details from the conversation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Transfer to humans" icon="phone-arrow-right">
    If the bot cannot help, it can transfer the call to a real person -- either a specific extension or a queue of agents.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Collect information" icon="clipboard-list">
    The bot can ask for and record details like names, phone numbers, booking references, and more.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  You can have multiple bots for different purposes. For example, one bot for daytime calls that transfers to agents, and another for after-hours calls that takes messages and creates tickets.
</Tip>
