## What is a deal?

A deal is a business opportunity that has a money amount attached to it.

Think of it this way: a **lead** is a person who might buy. A **deal** is the actual sale you are trying to close with them, and it has a specific value — for example, "Annual phone system subscription — Rs. 2,40,000."

Deals move through stages as you get closer to closing them. You can see your entire sales pipeline at a glance and know exactly how much revenue is in play.

## Opening the Deals page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Mini CRM in the sidebar">
    In the left sidebar, find the **Mini CRM** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Deals">
    Click **Deals**. You will land on the Deals page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The value cards

At the very top of the Deals page, you will see summary cards:

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Pipeline** | The total value of all your open deals added together. This is how much money is currently "in play." |
| **Won** | The total value of all deals that have been moved to the **Won** stage. This is money you have already closed. |

<Tip>
  Check these cards regularly. A healthy pipeline should always have more value than what you have already won. If your pipeline is empty, it is time to generate more leads.
</Tip>

## The deal pipeline (Kanban board)

The Deals page shows a Kanban board — just like the Leads page, but with different stages. Each column represents a stage in your sales process.

The default stages (from left to right) are:

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Lead** | A new opportunity. You know there is potential, but you have not evaluated it yet. |
| **Qualified** | You have confirmed the opportunity is real — the customer has a budget, a need, and a timeline. |
| **Proposal** | You have sent a proposal or quote to the customer. |
| **Negotiation** | The customer is reviewing your proposal. You are discussing terms, pricing, or scope. |
| **Won** | The deal is closed. The customer has said yes and the contract is signed. |
| **Lost** | The deal is dead. The customer went with someone else, or the opportunity disappeared. |

Each deal appears as a **card** showing the deal title, value, and associated company.

## Adding a new deal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Add Deal">
    On the Deals page, click the **Add Deal** button. A form appears.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the deal details">
    The form has the following fields:

    | Field | What to enter | Required? |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Title** | A short name for the deal (e.g. "GE Annual Subscription") | Yes |
    | **Stage** | Which pipeline stage this deal starts in (defaults to "Lead") | Yes |
    | **Amount** | The deal value in rupees (e.g. 2,40,000) | No |
    | **Company** | The company this deal is with — pick from your existing clients | No |
    | **Contact** | The specific person at the company you are dealing with | No |
    | **Expected Close Date** | When you expect this deal to close | No |
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Save">
    Click **Save**. The deal appears as a card in the column matching the stage you selected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Always fill in the **Amount** field, even if it is an estimate. This is what powers the Pipeline and Won value cards at the top. Without amounts, those totals will not be accurate.
</Note>

## Moving deals through stages

### On the Kanban board

Click and hold a deal card, then drag it to a different column. Drop it to move the deal to that stage.

As a deal progresses through your sales process:

1. **Lead** — you just identified the opportunity
2. **Qualified** — you confirmed the customer is serious
3. **Proposal** — you sent them a quote
4. **Negotiation** — you are working out the final details
5. **Won** — congratulations, the deal is closed
6. **Lost** — the deal did not work out

<Info>
  When you drag a deal to the **Won** column, its value is added to the **Won** total at the top of the page and removed from the **Pipeline** total. This gives you an accurate picture of closed revenue vs. pending revenue.
</Info>

## Viewing deals as a list

If you prefer working with a table instead of a Kanban board, switch to **List view** using the toggle at the top of the page.

The list view shows each deal as a row with columns for:

- **Title** — the deal name
- **Stage** — which pipeline stage it is in
- **Amount** — the deal value
- **Company** — the associated company
- **Contact** — the associated contact
- **Expected Close Date** — when you expect to close it

<Tip>
  List view is great for sorting and filtering. Click any column header to sort the table. For example, click **Amount** to sort deals from largest to smallest, so you can focus on your biggest opportunities first.
</Tip>

## Linking deals to companies and contacts

When you create a deal and link it to a company, that deal appears in the company's detail panel on the Clients page. This means:

- Open a company's profile, and you see all their deals
- Open a deal, and you see which company it belongs to
- Everything stays connected

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a deal">
    Click any deal card on the Kanban board or any row in the list view.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the Company and Contact fields">
    In the detail panel, you will see the **Company** and **Contact** fields. If they are empty, click to add a company or contact. If they are already filled, click to jump to that company or contact's profile.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  A deal without a linked company is harder to track. Always associate deals with a company so your team can see the full picture when they open a client's profile.
</Warning>
