## What is a lead?

A lead is a person who might become a customer. Maybe they called your business and asked about your services. Maybe they filled out a form on your website. Maybe someone on your team met them at an event.

The point is: they are interested, but they have not bought anything yet. Your job is to follow up, build the relationship, and try to turn them into a paying customer.

The Leads section helps you track every lead and see exactly where they are in the process.

## Opening the Leads page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Mini CRM in the sidebar">
    In the left sidebar, find the **Mini CRM** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Leads">
    Click **Leads**. You will land on the Leads page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Two ways to view your leads

The Leads page has two views. You can switch between them at any time using the toggle at the top of the page.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Kanban board" icon="columns-3">
    A visual board with columns for each stage. Each lead is a card. Drag cards between columns to change their status. Great for seeing the big picture at a glance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="List view" icon="table">
    A traditional table with one row per lead. Shows all details in columns. Great for filtering, sorting, and working through leads quickly.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The Kanban board

The Kanban board is the default view. It looks like a series of vertical columns, and each column represents a stage in your lead pipeline.

The default stages (from left to right) are:

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| **New** | A brand-new lead. You have not reached out yet. |
| **Contacted** | You have made initial contact — called them, emailed them, or messaged them. |
| **Qualified** | You have spoken with them and confirmed they are a genuine potential customer. |
| **Converted** | They have become a customer. The lead is won. |
| **Lost** | They decided not to buy, or you could not reach them. The lead is lost. |

Each lead appears as a **card** in one of these columns. The card shows the lead's name, company, and other key details.

<Tip>
  To move a lead to the next stage, simply **drag the card** from one column and **drop it** into the next column. For example, when you make first contact with a new lead, drag their card from "New" to "Contacted."
</Tip>

## Adding a new lead

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Add Lead">
    On the Leads page, click the **Add Lead** button. A form appears.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the lead details">
    The form has the following fields:

    | Field | What to enter | Required? |
    |---|---|---|
    | **First Name** | The lead's first name (e.g. "Priya") | Yes |
    | **Last Name** | The lead's last name (e.g. "Sharma") | Yes |
    | **Email** | Their email address | No |
    | **Phone** | Their phone number | No |
    | **Job Title** | Their role at their company (e.g. "Operations Manager") | No |
    | **Lead Source** | Where this lead came from (see list below) | No |
    | **Company** | The company they work for — pick from your existing clients or type a new one | No |
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Save">
    Click **Save**. The new lead appears as a card in the **New** column of the Kanban board.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Lead sources

When adding a lead, you can pick where they came from. This helps you track which channels are bringing in the most leads.

| Source | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Website** | They found you through your website |
| **Phone** | They called your business |
| **Referral** | Someone recommended you to them |
| **Social** | They came from social media (LinkedIn, Instagram, etc.) |
| **Advertisement** | They responded to an ad |
| **Cold Call** | Your team called them proactively |
| **Event** | You met them at a conference, trade show, or meetup |

<Info>
  Tracking lead sources helps you understand what is working. If most of your converted leads come from referrals, you know to invest more in referral programs.
</Info>

## Moving leads between stages

### On the Kanban board

Click and hold a lead card, then drag it to a different column. Release it to drop it into the new stage.

For example:
- You just spoke with a lead on the phone. Drag their card from **New** to **Contacted**.
- After a meeting, you confirmed they have budget and need your product. Drag from **Contacted** to **Qualified**.
- They signed a contract. Drag from **Qualified** to **Converted**.

### In the list view

Click the lead's row to open the detail panel. Find the **Stage** field and click on it. Pick the new stage from the dropdown.

## Assigning leads to team members

If you have multiple people on your sales team, you can assign each lead to a specific person.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lead">
    Click a lead card on the Kanban board, or click a row in the list view. The detail panel opens.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the Assigned To field">
    In the detail panel, look for the **Assigned To** field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick a team member">
    Click the field and select a person from the dropdown. That person is now responsible for following up with this lead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Assigning leads makes sure every potential customer has someone actively working on them. Unassigned leads tend to get forgotten.
</Note>

## Quick reference
