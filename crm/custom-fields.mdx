## Why customise?

Every business is different. A hotel's sales process does not look like a software company's. The default pipeline stages and fields might not cover everything you need.

The Customize section lets you change two things:

1. **Pipelines** — add, rename, reorder, or delete the stages that leads and deals move through
2. **Custom Fields** — add extra fields to your contacts, companies, or deals so you can track whatever matters to your business

## Opening the Customize page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Mini CRM in the sidebar">
    In the left sidebar, find the **Mini CRM** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Customize">
    Click **Customize**. You will land on the Customize page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You will see **two tabs** at the top of the page: **Pipelines** and **Custom Fields**. Click either tab to switch between them.

## Pipelines

Pipelines define the stages that your leads and deals move through. By default, leads have stages like New, Contacted, Qualified, etc. Deals have stages like Lead, Qualified, Proposal, etc.

You can change all of this to match your actual sales process.

### Adding a new stage

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Pipelines tab">
    On the Customize page, click the **Pipelines** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick which pipeline to edit">
    You will see two pipelines listed: **Leads Pipeline** and **Deals Pipeline**. Click the one you want to change.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add Stage">
    At the bottom of the stage list, click the **Add Stage** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name the stage">
    Type a name for the new stage. For example, if you are editing the Leads pipeline and your process includes a demo step, you might add a stage called "Demo Scheduled" between Contacted and Qualified.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The new stage appears in the pipeline and shows up as a new column on the Kanban board.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Renaming a stage

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the stage in the list">
    On the Pipelines tab, click the pipeline you want to edit. You will see all existing stages listed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click the stage name">
    Click directly on the stage name text. It becomes editable.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Type the new name and save">
    Type the new name and click **Save**. The stage is renamed everywhere — on the Kanban board, in list views, and in detail panels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Reordering stages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the pipeline">
    On the Pipelines tab, click the pipeline you want to edit.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Drag stages to reorder">
    Click and hold a stage, then drag it up or down in the list to change its position. The order here matches the order of columns on the Kanban board, from left to right.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to apply the new order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Deleting a stage

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the pipeline">
    On the Pipelines tab, click the pipeline you want to edit.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click the delete icon">
    Next to the stage you want to remove, click the **delete icon** (trash can).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm deletion">
    A confirmation dialog appears. Click **Delete** to confirm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If there are leads or deals currently in the stage you are deleting, you will be asked to move them to another stage first. You cannot delete a stage that still has items in it.
</Warning>

## Custom Fields

Custom fields let you add extra data fields to your records. The default fields (name, phone, email, etc.) cover the basics. Custom fields let you track anything else.

### Where custom fields can be added

You can add custom fields to three types of records:

| Record type | Examples of custom fields you might add |
|---|---|
| **Contacts** | Preferred language, birthday, loyalty tier |
| **Companies** | Contract number, account manager, renewal date |
| **Deals** | Product category, license type, discount percentage |

### Available field types

When you create a custom field, you pick a **type** that determines what kind of data the field holds.

| Type | What it is | Example use |
|---|---|---|
| **Text** | A single line of text | Contract number, department name |
| **Number** | A numeric value | Employee count, discount percentage |
| **Date** | A calendar date | Renewal date, onboarding date |
| **Select** | A dropdown with predefined options | Region (North, South, East, West) |
| **Checkbox** | A true/false toggle | "NDA signed?" or "VIP customer?" |
| **Email** | An email address (with validation) | Secondary email, billing email |
| **Phone** | A phone number (with validation) | WhatsApp number, alternate phone |
| **URL** | A web link | LinkedIn profile, company portal |
| **Long Text** | A multi-line text area | Special instructions, detailed notes |

### Adding a custom field

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Custom Fields tab">
    On the Customize page, click the **Custom Fields** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick the record type">
    You will see three sections: **Contacts**, **Companies**, and **Deals**. Click the section where you want to add a field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add Field">
    Click the **Add Field** button. A form appears.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure the field">
    Fill in the following:

    - **Field Name** — what this field is called (e.g. "Contract Number")
    - **Field Type** — pick from the types listed above (e.g. "Text")
    - **Required** — toggle this on if this field must be filled in when creating a record (leave off for optional fields)

    If you pick **Select** as the type, you will also need to add the dropdown options. Type each option and press Enter to add it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Save">
    Click **Save**. The custom field now appears on every record of that type. For example, if you added a "Contract Number" field to Companies, every company form and detail panel will now show that field.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Example: adding a "Contract Number" field to Companies

Here is a real-world example to walk you through it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Customize">
    Sidebar > Mini CRM > Customize.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click the Custom Fields tab">
    Click **Custom Fields** at the top of the page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Companies">
    Under the Custom Fields tab, click the **Companies** section.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add Field">
    Click the **Add Field** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    - **Field Name**: Contract Number
    - **Field Type**: Text
    - **Required**: Off (not every company will have a contract number right away)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Save">
    Click **Save**. Done. Now when you open any company record or add a new company, you will see a "Contract Number" field in the form.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Custom fields apply to **all** records of that type. When you add a field to Companies, it shows up on every company — not just one. If you only need to store a note for one specific company, use the Notes field instead.
</Info>

<Note>
  You can edit or delete custom fields later from the same Customize page. Deleting a custom field removes it from all records, and any data stored in that field is lost. Be careful.
</Note>

|---|---|

| Delete a stage | Trash icon next to the stage |
