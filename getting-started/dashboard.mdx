After you sign in, you land on your dashboard. This is your home base -- the first thing you see every time you open Astradial. This guide explains every part of the screen so you know exactly what you are looking at.

## The onboarding wizard

The very first time you log in after your organisation is approved, you will see an **onboarding wizard** at the top of your dashboard. This wizard walks you through the four essential steps to get your phone system up and running.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Users" icon="users">
    Add the people on your team who will be using Astradial. Each user gets their own login and extension number. You need at least one user (besides yourself) to start receiving calls.

    Click the button in this step and it will take you straight to the Users page where you can add team members.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get a Number" icon="phone">
    Buy a phone number (called a DID) for your organisation. This is the number your customers will call. You can buy local numbers for different regions.

    Click this step and it takes you to the Phone Numbers page to browse and purchase available numbers.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Routing" icon="route">
    Tell Astradial what to do when someone calls your number. You can route calls to a specific user, to a queue, or to an AI bot. This is where you set up the flow of incoming calls.

    Click this step to go to your phone number's routing settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Go Live" icon="rocket">
    Once your users are created, your number is purchased, and your routing is configured, you are ready to go live. Make a test call to your new number and make sure everything works.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You do not have to complete the wizard in one sitting. The steps stay at the top of your dashboard until you finish them all. You can come back to it any time.
</Tip>

## Stats cards

Below the onboarding wizard (or at the top if you have already completed it), you will see a row of **stats cards**. These give you a quick snapshot of what is happening in your organisation right now.

| Card | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Total Calls** | The total number of calls your organisation has received and made. This counts all calls -- answered, missed, and busy. |
| **Active Users** | How many users in your organisation are currently online and available to take calls. |
| **Open Tickets** | The number of support tickets that are still open and need attention. |
| **Avg Duration** | The average length of your calls. This helps you understand how long typical conversations last. |

<Info>
  These numbers update automatically. You do not need to refresh the page to see the latest stats.
</Info>

## Call Volume chart

Below the stats cards, you will see a **Call Volume** chart. This is a visual graph that shows you how many calls you received over time.

- The **horizontal axis** (bottom) shows the time period -- days or hours depending on the range you select.
- The **vertical axis** (left side) shows the number of calls.
- You can hover over any point on the chart to see the exact number of calls for that time period.

This chart helps you spot patterns. For example, you might notice that most of your calls come in on Monday mornings, which means you should make sure extra agents are available at that time.

## Recent Calls table

Below the chart, you will see a **Recent Calls** table. This shows your most recent calls in a list format.

Each row in the table shows:

| Column | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **From** | The phone number or extension that made the call. |
| **To** | The phone number or extension that received the call. |
| **Status** | Whether the call was answered, missed, busy, or failed. |
| **Duration** | How long the call lasted (in minutes and seconds). |
| **Time** | When the call happened. |

<Tip>
  Click on any call in the table to see more details about it, including the full call path and recording (if recording is enabled).
</Tip>

To see your complete call history going further back, click **Calls** in the sidebar navigation. The dashboard only shows the most recent calls.

## Sidebar navigation

On the left side of your screen, you will see the **sidebar**. This is how you navigate to every part of Astradial. The sidebar is organised into sections, each containing related pages.

### Configure

This section is where you set up the core pieces of your phone system.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Users" icon="users" href="/users/add-users">
    Add and manage the people on your team. Each user gets a login, an extension number, and a role that controls what they can access.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Phone Numbers (DIDs)" icon="phone" href="/phone-numbers/buy-a-number">
    Buy new phone numbers, see your existing numbers, and configure what happens when someone calls each number.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Trunks" icon="tower-broadcast">
    Advanced SIP trunk configuration. Most users do not need to touch this -- it is set up for you automatically.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Queues" icon="people-group" href="/queues/create-queue">
    Create and manage call queues. A queue holds callers in a waiting line until an agent is free to answer.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Monitor

This section is where you keep an eye on what is happening with your calls.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Calls" icon="phone-arrow-down-left" href="/calls/call-history">

  <Card title="Live" icon="signal-stream" href="/calls/live-monitoring">

<CardGroup cols={1}>

## Navigating the dashboard

Here are a few tips that apply everywhere in Astradial, not just the dashboard home page.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I get back to the dashboard?">
    Click the **Astradial logo** at the top of the sidebar, or click **Dashboard** in the sidebar. Either one takes you back to the home page with your stats and recent calls.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I collapse the sidebar?">
    Yes. On smaller screens the sidebar may automatically collapse. You can click the menu icon at the top to expand or collapse it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I sign out?">
    Look for your profile icon or name at the bottom of the sidebar. Click on it and you will see a **Sign Out** option.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I switch between organisations?">
    If you belong to more than one organisation, you can switch between them from the organisation selector at the top of the sidebar. Click on your organisation name to see the list.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
