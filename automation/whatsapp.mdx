## What is the WhatsApp integration?

The WhatsApp integration lets Astradial send WhatsApp messages on your behalf. Instead of manually texting customers, the system does it for you -- automatically.

Think of it like giving Astradial the ability to text your customers on WhatsApp whenever something happens. A customer missed a call? WhatsApp message. A ticket was resolved? WhatsApp notification. A booking was confirmed? WhatsApp receipt.

The integration works through **MSG91**, which is the service that actually delivers the WhatsApp messages. You connect your MSG91 account to Astradial, and then Astradial can send messages through it.

<Info>
  You need a **MSG91 account** with WhatsApp Business API access to use this integration. If you do not have one, visit [msg91.com](https://msg91.com) to sign up.
</Info>

## How to connect WhatsApp

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the WhatsApp page">
    In the left sidebar, click **Automate**, then click **WhatsApp**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your MSG91 credentials">
    You will see a form asking for your MSG91 API credentials. You need two things from your MSG91 account:

    - **API Key** -- found in your MSG91 dashboard under API settings
    - **Sender ID / WhatsApp Number** -- the WhatsApp Business number registered with MSG91

    Paste these into the form.

    <Tip>
      If you are not sure where to find your MSG91 API key, log in to MSG91, go to **API** in the top menu, and copy the key from there.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save and verify">
    Click **Save**. Astradial will verify the connection by checking your API key with MSG91.

    If everything is correct, you will see a green **Connected** status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Keep your MSG91 API key safe. Anyone with this key can send messages from your WhatsApp Business number. Do not share it publicly.
</Warning>

## Sending WhatsApp messages

Once connected, you can send WhatsApp messages from two places:

### From tickets

When you are viewing a ticket, you can send a WhatsApp message directly to the customer associated with that ticket. This is useful for follow-ups, status updates, or asking for more information.

### From workflows

The real power of WhatsApp is in automation. When you create a workflow, you can add a **Send WhatsApp** action that fires automatically when a trigger happens.

**Common automations:**
- Missed call -- send "Sorry we missed your call, we will call you back"
- Ticket resolved -- send "Your issue has been resolved, reference number #1234"
- Appointment confirmed -- send "Your booking is confirmed for tomorrow at 3 PM"

See [Workflows](/automation/workflows) for how to set up automated WhatsApp messages.

## WhatsApp templates

WhatsApp Business requires you to use **pre-approved message templates** for outbound messages. You cannot send free-form text to customers who have not messaged you first -- this is a WhatsApp rule, not an Astradial limitation.

<Note>
  Templates are created and approved in your MSG91 account, not inside Astradial. Once a template is approved by MSG91 (and by WhatsApp), it becomes available for use in Astradial workflows and tickets.
</Note>

### What is a template?

A template is a pre-written message with placeholders for dynamic content. For example:

```
Hi {{name}}, we missed your call at {{time}}.
We will call you back shortly. — {{company_name}}
```

When the message is sent, the placeholders get filled in with real values:

```
Hi Priya, we missed your call at 2:45 PM.
We will call you back shortly. — Astradial
```

## Configure ticket status notifications

You can set up automatic WhatsApp notifications for ticket status changes. When a ticket moves from "Open" to "In Progress" or "Resolved", the customer gets a WhatsApp message.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a workflow">
    Go to **Automate** then **Workflows** and create a new workflow.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the trigger">
    Select **Ticket Created** or **Ticket Updated** as the trigger.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a Send WhatsApp action">
    Add a **Send WhatsApp** action. Select the template you want to use for the notification and map the ticket fields (customer name, ticket number, status) to the template placeholders.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save and activate">
    Save the workflow and turn on the toggle. Now customers will be automatically notified via WhatsApp whenever their ticket status changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Start with just one notification template (like "ticket resolved") and see how customers respond. You can always add more templates and automations later.
</Tip>
