## What is a ticket?

A ticket is a to-do item that the system creates when something needs follow-up.

Here is the simplest way to think about it: when a customer calls your business and nobody answers, that call should not just disappear. Instead, Astradial creates a **ticket** automatically so your team knows they need to call that person back.

Tickets are not just for missed calls. They are created whenever something happens that needs attention:

- **Missed calls** — someone called and nobody picked up
- **Bot interactions** — an AI bot spoke with a caller and flagged something for a human to review
- **Queue timeouts** — a caller waited in a queue too long and hung up before reaching an agent

<Info>
  You do not need to create tickets manually (although you can). The system creates them for you automatically based on the events above. Your job is to review them and take action.
</Info>

## Where to find tickets

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the sidebar">
    Look at the left side of your screen. You will see the sidebar menu with several sections.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Monitor">
    In the sidebar, find the **Monitor** section. Click on **Tickets**.

    You will land on the Tickets page, which shows all tickets for your organisation in a table.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the badge">
    Notice the small number badge next to the word **Tickets** in the sidebar. That number tells you how many **open** tickets you currently have. If the badge says **3**, you have three tickets waiting for attention.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What is inside a ticket?

Every ticket contains these fields:

| Field | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Caller number** | The phone number of the person who called. This is the number you would call back. |
| **Category** | What type of event created this ticket — for example, "Missed Call" or "Bot Interaction." |
| **Summary** | A short one-line description of what happened. For example: "Missed call from +91 98765 43210." |
| **Details** | The full story. For bot interactions, this includes what the caller said and what the bot said. |
| **Priority** | How urgent this ticket is — **Low**, **Medium**, **High**, or **Urgent**. |
| **Status** | Where this ticket is in the workflow — **Open**, **In Progress**, or **Closed**. |

<Tip>
  New tickets always start with a status of **Open**. When someone on your team starts working on it, they change it to **In Progress**. When the issue is resolved, they mark it as **Closed**.
</Tip>

## How tickets help your business

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Never miss a callback" icon="phone-missed">
    Every missed call becomes a ticket. Your team sees it, calls the customer back, and closes the ticket. No call slips through.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Track bot conversations" icon="robot">
    When your AI bot talks to a customer and cannot fully resolve the issue, a ticket is created with the full conversation. Your team picks up right where the bot left off.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Monitor queue performance" icon="clock">
    If callers are hanging up before reaching an agent, queue timeout tickets tell you exactly when and how often that is happening.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Measure your team" icon="chart-line">
    See how many tickets are open, how quickly they get resolved, and who is handling them. Spot problems before they get worse.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
