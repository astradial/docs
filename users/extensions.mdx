## What is an extension?

An extension is a short number -- like `1001` or `1002` -- that identifies a person in your organisation. Think of it like a room number in a building. If you want to reach someone, you dial their extension.

Every user in Astradial gets a unique extension when they are added. No two people can have the same extension, just like no two rooms have the same number.

<Tip>
  Extensions are only used for internal calls within your organisation. When someone calls your business from the outside, the call comes into your phone number (DID) and Astradial routes it to the right extension.
</Tip>

## What is a softphone?

To actually make and receive calls, your team members need a **softphone app**. A softphone is just a phone app that runs on a computer or mobile phone instead of a physical desk phone.

Popular softphone apps include:

- **Zoiper** (Windows, Mac, iOS, Android) -- free version available
- **Opal** (iOS, Android) -- simple and clean
- **Opal VoIP** -- lightweight mobile option
- **Opal SIP Client** -- another mobile-friendly option

<Info>
  You can use any SIP-compatible softphone app. The ones listed above are just recommendations. If you already have a softphone you like, it will probably work with Astradial.
</Info>

## Connection details

To connect a softphone to Astradial, you need three pieces of information:

| Detail             | What it is                                                       | Example              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| **SIP server**     | The address of your Astradial PBX server                         | `pbx.astradial.com`  |
| **Extension**      | The user's extension number                                      | `1001`               |
| **SIP password**   | The password set when the user was created                       | (set during setup)   |

<Note>
  The SIP password is the same password you set when you added the user. If you forgot it, an admin can reset it from the Users page.
</Note>

## Setting up Zoiper (step by step)

Here is how to set up Zoiper, one of the most popular softphone apps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download Zoiper">
    Go to [zoiper.com](https://www.zoiper.com) and download the app for your device (Windows, Mac, iOS, or Android). Install it and open it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a SIP account">
    When Zoiper opens, it will ask you to set up an account. Choose **SIP account** (not IAX or other options).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the server address">
    In the **Domain** or **Server** field, enter your Astradial SIP server address. Your admin will provide this -- it looks something like `pbx.astradial.com`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your extension and password">
    - In the **Username** field, enter your extension number (e.g. `1001`)
    - In the **Password** field, enter your SIP password
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register">
    Click **Register** or **Save**. Zoiper will try to connect to the server. If everything is correct, you will see a green status or a "Registered" message. That means you are connected and ready to make calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test it">
    Try calling another extension in your organisation. Pick up the softphone, dial their extension number (e.g. `1002`), and hit call. If their softphone rings, everything is working.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

<Warning>
  If your softphone says "Registration failed" or "Forbidden", double-check these things:
  - Is the SIP server address correct? No typos?
  - Is the extension number correct?
  - Is the password correct? Passwords are case-sensitive.
  - Is your internet connection working?
</Warning>

<Tip>
  If you are on a company network with a firewall, SIP traffic might be blocked. Ask your IT team to make sure SIP ports (5060 UDP and 5060 TCP) are open.
</Tip>
